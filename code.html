Experience at Amazon

After a year of learning how to code, I was fortunate to get an internship at Amazon in Seattle, Washington. During the internship I was able to increase productivity for the Content Aquisition Management Team by over 100%.

Prime Video has two types of content providers: contracted providers, like HBO or Paramount, who might have hundreds of titles they would want on prime video, and individual providers, independent filmmakers who have a handful of titles they would like to publish on prime video. In order for those indie filmmakers to publish videos to prime video, we would have to grant them permissions to publish titles manually. This manual process would require a request from the filmmaker, and then, the CAM team would have to manually create an authorized account for the filmmaker to use. This would include setting the territories in which they could publish, how long their account would have permissions, etc. From the time the filmmaker had indicated they wanted a PVD account to when they actually got it, it would be about two weeks.

In order to fix this, we created a system which we called PVD tokens. Let's say a user wants to create a prime video account to distribute their We created an internal form UI which the CAM team could fill out based on information provided by the content creator. When a content creator indicated they wanted to create an account, all the CAM team needed to do was review the information and fill out a form. This form generated a unique "token", which was emailed to the content provider. Using this "token", the content provider could create a prime video direct account already set up and begin uploading videos to Prime.
